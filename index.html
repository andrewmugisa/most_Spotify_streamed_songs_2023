Business Context

Music streaming has transformed how people discover music, with Spotify dominating the industry. Artists, producers, and labels need to understand what actually drives streaming success.

This project tests whether musical attributes tempo, energy, danceability or distribution factors like playlist placement predict a song's stream count.

Analysis
Playlist Count
Strongest predictor. Tracks in more playlists accumulate far more streams the clearest signal in the data.
Danceability
Mild positive trend (r ≈ −0.11). High variance not a reliable standalone predictor.
Release Timing
Older catalog tracks average higher streams due to longer accumulation time.
Energy & BPM
No meaningful correlation. Null findings tempo and intensity do not predict streaming success.
Stream Distribution
Heavily right-skewed. Most tracks fall below 400M streams; a handful exceed 2B.
Playlist Appearances vs. Average Streams
Tracks in more playlists consistently accumulate more streams the strongest signal in the dataset
Correlation of Audio Features with Streams
Pearson r  ·  green = positive, red = negative relationship with stream count
Average Streams by Release Year
Older catalog tracks score higher more time to accumulate. 2023 releases appear low but are simply newer.
Limitations
  • Playlist causality is unclear popularity may drive playlist placement, not the reverse
  • No artist-level features in the dataset
  • Stream counts are cumulative, not 2023-only
  • Only top-streamed tracks no baseline for average songs
Next Steps
  • Run regression: playlist count + song age → streams
  • Investigate artist-level patterns in high-stream outliers
  • Test playlist placement causality with time-series data
  • Compare findings across genres or global regions
